Diffusion Models for Recommender Systems

From Content Distribution → Content Creation

KDD 2025 Lecture‑Style Tutorial
August 4, 2025 · Toronto, Canada (KDD 2025)

Overview

Diffusion models have revolutionised generative imaging and audio, but their impact on personalised recommendation has only recently started to unfold. In this tutorial we bridge the gap between diffusion-based generation and classical collaborative filtering, presenting a unifying view that treats recommendation as probabilistic denoising over user–item interaction manifolds. We cover:

  • Score‑based formulations of user preference modelling
  • Conditional diffusion for multi‑modal item understanding
  • Diffusion‑driven content creation loops that close the gap between generation and ranking
  • Scalable training strategies on billion‑scale interaction logs
  • Open challenges at the intersection of recommendation, generative modelling, and copyright

Detailed Outline

  1. Primer on Diffusion Models – score matching, SDE formulation, guidance techniques.
  2. Bridging Diffusion & Recommendation – user–item graphs as noisy channels; denoising as ranking.
  3. Architectures – text/image encoders, cross‑attention fusion, time embedding tricks.
  4. Training at Scale – negative sampling vs. continuous noise, importance weighting, distributed optimisation.
  5. Evaluation Protocols – offline metrics, simulation with user behaviour models, human preference studies.
  6. Content Creation Loop – generating new items, online AB testing, feedback alignment.
  7. Open Challenges – safety, bias, IP issues, and next‑generation personalised creativity.
