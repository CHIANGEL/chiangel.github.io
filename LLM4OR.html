ORLM: A Customizable Framework in Training Large Models for Automated Optimization Modeling

Chenyu Huang, Zhengyang Tang, Shixi Hu, Ruoqing Jiang, Xin Zheng, Dongdong Ge, Benyou Wang, and Zizhuo Wang.

Published at Operations Research, 2025.

 >
TLDR

Trains open-source LLMs to write optimization formulations and solver code from problem descriptions.

Abstract
Optimization modeling is essential for applying OR in practice but usually requires expert modeling knowledge. ORLM addresses the shortage of high-quality training data by introducing OR-Instruct, a semi-automated synthesis framework, and IndustryOR, an industrial benchmark. The trained 7B-scale ORLMs improve automated modeling across NL4Opt, MAMO, and IndustryOR, while the paper also discusses scaling, reinforcement learning, and industrial human-machine workflows....

Dynamic programming requires state, action, transition, and reward design, making it different from standard LP/MIP formulation. This work introduces DP-Bench for systematic evaluation and DPLM, a 7B specialized model. Its DualReflect data pipeline combines forward generation for diversity with backward generation for reliability, showing that the two modes are complementary across data regimes....

Solver-Informed RL: Grounding Large Language Models for Authentic Optimization Modeling

Yitian Chen, Jingfan Xia, Siyu Shao, Dongdong Ge, and Yinyu Ye.

Published at NeurIPS, 2025.

 >
TLDR

Uses optimization solvers as verifiers to train LLMs with executable, feasible, objective-aware rewards.

Abstract
LLMs can automate formulation but often produce hallucinated or unusable models. SIRL applies reinforcement learning with verifiable reward by using external optimization solvers to assess executable code and LP-file representations. Solver feedback on syntax, feasibility, and solution quality becomes reward signal, and also supports self-consistency data synthesis. Experiments show strong improvements in accurate and executable optimization modeling....

StepORLM: A Self-Evolving Framework with Generative Process Supervision for Operations Research Language Models

Chenyu Zhou, Tianyi Xu, Jianghao Lin, and Dongdong Ge.

Published at ICLR, 2026.

 >
TLDR

Aligns OR language models with holistic process critique rather than only final-answer rewards.

Abstract
Outcome rewards can reinforce flawed reasoning, and local process rewards miss dependencies across modeling steps. StepORLM introduces a self-evolving framework with a policy model and a generative process reward model that improve together. It combines solver-based outcome verification with holistic GenPRM process evaluation and aligns the policy through W-DPO, establishing strong results across multiple OR benchmarks....

Submitted to Operations Research.

 >
TLDR

Combines multi-agent debate with reusable solver-verified memory for more reliable optimization modeling.

Abstract
Agora-Opt addresses the difficulty of solving optimization modeling tasks from natural language by combining decentralized debate with a read-write memory bank. Multiple teams independently produce end-to-end solutions, reconcile disagreements through outcome-grounded debate, and store solver-verified artifacts and debate traces. The framework improves over strong zero-shot, training-centric, and prior agentic baselines while remaining flexible across model backbones....

 >
TLDR

Uses a structured agent workflow to formulate large-scale optimization models with external data inputs.

Abstract
LEAN-LLM-OPT targets labor-intensive large-scale optimization formulation by orchestrating LLM agents over a problem description and associated datasets. The design reduces planning and data-handling burden, performs strongly in simulations, and introduces Large-Scale-OR and Air-NRM for large-scale optimization auto-formulation....

Yitian Chen, Cheng Cheng, Yinan Sun, Zi Ling, and Dongdong Ge.

Accepted by ICML, 2026.

 >
TLDR

Builds a controllable benchmark engine to diagnose where LLMs fail as optimization tasks scale.

Abstract
OPT-Engine studies the boundaries of LLM capability in automated optimization modeling. It provides an extensible benchmark framework with controllable and scalable difficulty across ten canonical OR tasks, including LP and MIP families. Experiments analyze robustness under out-of-distribution complexity and identify major bottlenecks, showing that tool-integrated reasoning remains more robust while constraint formulation is a central failure point....

Working in progress.

 >
TLDR

Evolves interpretable inventory policies and deploys only statistically certified improvements.

Abstract
AlphaInventory adapts LLM-based evolutionary search to online, non-stationary inventory settings. The framework trains an LLM with reinforcement learning, uses demand as well as numerical and textual features, and generates white-box policies screened by confidence-interval certification. A theoretical interface connects training, inference, and deployment, while experiments on synthetic and retail data show gains over classical and deep-learning baselines....
